Small things worth writing down

21 June 2026 · ~2 min

I've started keeping this notes page for a reason that's slightly embarrassing: I kept re-deriving the same small results. A change of variables I'd figured out in March would be gone by June, and I'd sit there reconstructing it from scratch, mildly annoyed at my past self for not writing it down.

So this is the fix. Not a polished blog, not a portfolio — a notebook that happens to be public. The bar for posting is deliberately low: if a thing took me more than ten minutes to work out and I'd be sad to lose it, it goes here.

The one rule

There's exactly one thing I try to hold myself to, borrowed from every good maths lecturer I've had:

Write the version you wish you'd found when you were stuck.

That means stating the trick plainly before dressing it up, showing at least one concrete example, and admitting where I'm still confused instead of papering over it. If a post can't clear that bar, it stays in my drafts.

Everything here is provisional and occasionally wrong. That's the point — a notebook you're afraid to be wrong in isn't much use. If something looks off, I'd genuinely like to hear about it.
